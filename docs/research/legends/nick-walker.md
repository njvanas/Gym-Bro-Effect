## 5. Draft summary + principles + guidelines (grounded in Tier B, reporting on Tier A footage)

### Summary (draft)
Current-generation mass monster ("The Mutant") who films and narrates most of his own training on his official YouTube channel. As of his most recently reported split (a 5-day program introduced in November 2024 under coach Kyle Wilkes), he prioritizes legs and chest twice weekly with 3-4 rest days per muscle group and deliberately avoids drop sets, rest-pause, forced reps, and partials in favor of full range-of-motion standard sets. He has changed coaches and splits multiple times in recent years, so this reflects a snapshot of his training, not a fixed lifelong system.

### Principles (draft)
- Full range of motion and a hard, deliberate muscle contraction take priority over intensity techniques (his own reported preference, avoiding drop sets/rest-pause/forced reps/partials).
- Legs and chest trained twice weekly; other muscle groups get 3-4 days of rest between sessions.
- Trains toward failure, not always to complete failure, to manage fatigue and injury risk on leg day specifically.
- Split and coaching have changed multiple times in recent years — adaptability over dogma.
- Mixed rep ranges within a single exercise (e.g., 2 heavy sets at 6-8 reps, then 2 lighter sets at 10-15 reps) rather than one fixed number.

### Guidelines (draft, per the Nov 2024 "Mutant Split")
- **Training days/week:** 5, with legs and chest each hit twice.
- **Frequency per muscle:** legs and chest 2x/week; other groups 1x/week with 3-4 rest days.
- **Warm-up protocol:** not documented with fixed percentages; leg day includes 2-3 acclimation sets at 10-12 reps around 80-90% toward failure before working sets.
- **Working-set protocol:** standard straight sets to near-failure, full range of motion; deliberately no drop sets/rest-pause/forced reps/partials.
- **Rep ranges:** heavy compound work 6-8 reps (2 sets), lighter follow-up work 10-15 reps (2 sets); most accessory/isolation work 12-15 reps.
