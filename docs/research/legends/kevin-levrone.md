## Verified Claims (Tier A/B only)

1. Levrone's own stated rep philosophy for compound lifts (B1): "I grow by going heavy," typically in the 6-8 rep range, rather than chasing 10+ reps.
2. Levrone's own stated push/pull split structure (B3): pushing movements grouped on one day, pulling movements on another — but no full weekly day count or leg-day placement is stated.
3. A specific, attributed triceps routine exists (B2): 4 exercises x 4 sets x 6-8 reps each (16 sets total) — One-Arm Dumbbell Triceps Extension, Rope Pushdown, Lying Triceps Extension, Close-Grip Bench Press.
4. Levrone's own stated reasoning on isolation/pushdown work (B2): higher reps aimed at "maximum burn," not pump.
5. He starred in his own comeback documentary (*Born to Overcome*, A1) and ran his own early YouTube channel ("The Levrone Report," A4 — confirmed via his own words in A2).

## Gaps — documented honestly

- **No full weekly split or day count is confirmed** beyond the two-way push/pull structure (B3). Leg-day placement, total training days/week, and frequency per muscle are not stated in any primary source reviewed.
- **No warm-up protocol is specified** in any source reviewed.
- **The "straight sets, no forced reps/drop sets" characterization is M&F's own editorial framing (B1), not a direct Levrone quote** — per the methodology's rule on distinguishing direct quotes from journalist paraphrase, this is **not** treated as verified, and `intensityTechniques` is left empty rather than asserting "no techniques used" as fact.
- ***Born to Overcome* (A1) was not transcribed for specific set/rep detail** in this pass — the strongest remaining lead, since it is a documentary he starred in with real training footage, structurally comparable to Yates' *Blood & Guts*.
- B2's own article contains **two internally inconsistent versions of the same triceps routine** (different pushdown rep counts/exercise order) — the version matching the article's own stated "16 sets" total was used; the discrepancy is noted rather than silently resolved.

## Draft Copy (Tier A/B only)

> Kevin Levrone has no single official DVD or branded program, but multiple Muscle & Fitness/Flex features quote him directly on his training approach, and he starred in his own 2018 comeback documentary, *Born to Overcome*. In his own words, he trains compound lifts heavy for 6-8 reps ("I grow by going heavy") and isolation/pushdown work for higher reps aimed at burn rather than pump, splitting pushing movements onto one day and pulling movements onto another. A dedicated Muscle & Fitness feature publishes his attributed triceps routine: four exercises, four sets of 6-8 reps each. Beyond this, his full weekly split, warm-up protocol, and use (or non-use) of beyond-failure intensity techniques are not confirmed in primary sources.
