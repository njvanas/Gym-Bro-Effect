## Verified Claims (Tier A/B only)

1. Platz's own stated rep philosophy: reps matter more than absolute weight, and he deliberately blended heavier/lower-rep days with higher-rep days (B2).
2. Platz's own account of a specific historical feat — squatting 405 lbs for 50 reps by mentally chunking the set into 10 blocks of 5, with a partner counting aloud — is confirmed in his own words across multiple outlets quoting the same Instagram clip (A3).
3. Platz's own stated mental approach: never leave the gym without beating his own prior best effort; deliberately sought discomfort every session (A2).
4. No official DVD, book, or branded program exists that publishes a verified sets/reps table for a full leg session or weekly split. Every specific table found is a Tier C reconstruction.

## Gaps — documented honestly

- **No verified day-by-day split, warm-up protocol, or full exercise list.** The A1 podcast (Shadow Talk with Dorian Yates) is the single highest-value unexplored lead — it is a 96-minute primary interview by another gold-standard-sourced legend, and was not transcribed in this pass.
- **The 405x50 anecdote is a historical feat, not a training template.** It describes one all-time set, not his normal working-set scheme; it should not be presented as "his rep range."
- **DigitalMuscle's 2017 "teaches the Squat" interview (B1) was not transcribed** — the second-highest-value lead for concrete technique/programming detail.
- Every specific sets/reps-per-exercise table attributed to Platz online (leg extension 5-8x10-15, hack squat 5x10-15, etc.) is uncited. `styles.json` should not present any of these as verified.
