## Verified Claims (Tier A/B only)

1. **"Stimulate, don't annihilate" is Haney's own, repeatedly self-stated motto**, explicitly meaning "don't train to failure" for injury/recovery reasons — confirmed in his own words across A2, A3, A6, and corroborated by eyewitness B1. `styles.json`'s principle "Stimulate the muscle — do not annihilate it every workout" is directly supported.
2. **Three-on/one-off is Haney's own stated favorite split**, explicitly because it gives "three full days to recover" while still allowing 75–85% of max (A4) — this is the direct source of the `guidelines.workingSetProtocol` "75-85%" figure and `trainingDaysPerWeek` "3 on, 1 off."
3. **Push/pull pairing (not muscle-group-of-similar-size pairing) is his stated joint-protection rationale**, explicitly contrasted against pairing two press days back to back (which he says caused his own shoulder problems) — confirmed almost verbatim in both A2 and A3, independently reported by two different outlets from what appears to be the same recent video appearance.
4. **Calves and abs trained every session** is confirmed in Haney's own push/pull breakdown (A2/A3: "that's one day, along with calves and abs") — matching `styles.json`'s "Train calves and abs every training day."
5. **Pre-exhaust technique use** (e.g., leg extensions before squats) is self-reported (A6) as part of his intensity approach — this is not currently reflected in `styles.json`'s `intensityTechniques` (which is empty for this style) and could be a candidate addition if the file is revised later.

These may be two different eras of the same underlying philosophy rather than one fixed program — `styles.json`'s single `splitOverview` (Chest & Arms / Legs / Back & Shoulders / Rest) matches the older Poliquin-documented version, not the push/pull-labeled 2025 phrasing. Worth flagging that Haney appears to describe his split slightly differently depending on which era/interview you catch him in.
- No book-page-level citation was obtained for *TotaLee Awesome*, *Ultimate Bodybuilding*, or *Beyond the Pump* in this pass (all are Kindle-only/out-of-print physical editions per leehaney.com's shop page) — the training-split and 75–85%-of-max detail is corroborated entirely through interviews/video rather than his own books. If deeper verification is ever needed, purchasing *TotaLee Awesome* would be the highest-value next step.

## Draft Copy (Tier A/B only)

> Lee Haney's eight-Olympia philosophy is best summarized in his own words: "stimulate, don't annihilate" — train hard enough to trigger growth without pushing to failure, because failure raises injury risk and slows the recovery that growth actually depends on. His stated favorite structure is three days on, one day off, specifically because it gives each muscle group three full recovery days while still allowing 75–85% of max loads. He pairs push and pull movements in the same session (e.g., push chest / pull biceps, or push shoulders / pull back) rather than stacking two press-heavy days back to back — a lesson he says he learned the hard way after his own shoulders were injured by training chest and shoulders together. Calves and abs are trained every session regardless of the day's focus.
