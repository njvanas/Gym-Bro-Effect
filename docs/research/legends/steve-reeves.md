## Verified claims (Tier A/B only)

1. **Training order, quoted directly from the book (via checkmeowt.co.uk):** train smaller upper-body muscles first, legs last, in this exact order: Deltoids → Pectorals → Lats → Biceps → Triceps → Quadriceps → Hamstrings → Calves → Lower back → Abdominals → Neck. His own stated reason: roughly 80% of blood sits in the legs/glutes at rest, so training legs first would divert blood away from the upper body for the rest of the session.
2. **Frequency and recovery, corroborated across the book review and multiple secondary sources:** full-body training 3x/week (Monday/Wednesday/Friday), with a day and a half of rest between sessions.
3. **Tempo and intensity, corroborated by an independent 1964 eyewitness (Grimek) plus the book:** slow, controlled reps — roughly a 2-second lift, 3-second lower, emphasizing the negative — taken close to failure with real concentration, not rushed or chatty between sets.
4. **A specific, book-attributed full-body exercise table** (per checkmeowt.co.uk's direct citation of the book) of roughly 20 exercises covering all 11 muscle groups in the stated order, mostly at 3 sets x 8-12 reps, opening with a 1x20 dumbbell-swing warmup and closing with partner-assisted neck flexion work.
5. **A separate, earlier self-authored magazine article** ("Classic Physique," *Your Physique*, May 1951) documents that Reeves was writing about his own full-body training methodology in his own words as early as 1951 — confirming the philosophy is decades-older than the 1995 book, though the exact numbers from this specific article are contested between two secondary reconstructions (see Gaps).
6. **Neck training was a deliberate, named part of his program** (both the book-review table and multiple secondary sources agree) — Reeves believed under- or over-developed necks both hurt overall proportion.

## Gaps — left as "Not yet verified from primary sources"

- The exact 1951 *Your Physique* "Classic Physique" article table is reconstructed by two secondary sites that disagree with each other on specific set counts for several exercises — neither reconstruction is treated as authoritative; the 1995-book-sourced table (checkmeowt.co.uk, which quotes the book directly) is used instead as the more reliably-attributed numeric table.
- The book's exact page-by-page text was not directly reviewed — the numbers here trace to a third-party review's direct quotations and its own transcription of the exercise table, not to a page scan.
- Rest-interval specifics (45-60s between sets, ~2min between exercises) are consistent across Tier B/C sources but not independently confirmed against a direct book quote in this pass.

## Draft copy for `styles.json`

**Summary:** anchor to *Building the Classic Physique — The Natural Way* (Steve Reeves with
George Helmer), his own self-authored training book, as reviewed and directly quoted by a
third-party source; note that a separate, earlier self-authored 1951 *Your Physique* magazine
article establishes the same full-body philosophy decades before the book, but that article's
exact exercise numbers are contested between two secondary reconstructions and are not used here.

**Principles (Tier A/B only):**
- Full-body training, 3x/week (Mon/Wed/Fri), with a day and a half of rest between sessions — never a split routine.
- Fixed exercise order every session, in his own stated logic: smaller upper-body muscles first, legs last, so leg training doesn't divert blood away from the upper body for the rest of the workout.
- Slow, controlled tempo (roughly 2 seconds up, 3 seconds down) with real concentration between sets, corroborated by an independent 1964 eyewitness account (John Grimek).
- Trained the neck as a deliberate, named part of the program — proportion, not maximum size, was the goal.
- Wrote about his own full-body training philosophy in the bodybuilding press as early as 1951, decades before publishing his full book in 1995 — a consistent, long-held position, not a late-career reinvention.

**Guidelines:**
- `trainingDaysPerWeek`: "3 (Monday/Wednesday/Friday), full body each session, with a day and a half of rest between workouts."
- `frequencyPerMuscle`: "Every session — each muscle group trained all 3 days/week, in a fixed order (see splitOverview)."
- `warmupProtocol`: "1 set of 20 dumbbell swings before the main session, per the book-review table."
- `workingSetProtocol`: "Mostly 3 sets of 8-12 reps per exercise, taken with a slow, controlled tempo; neck and calf work use higher reps (~15-20+) and lower set counts, per the book-review table."
- `repRanges`: Most exercises 8-12 reps; calves/warmup swings ~20 reps; neck work ~15 reps per side.
- `splitOverview`: single documented day-type = "Full body" (same fixed muscle-group order every session, 3x/week — not a rotating split).
