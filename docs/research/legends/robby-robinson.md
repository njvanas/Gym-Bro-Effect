## Follow-up deep dive (2026-07-24) — complete Heavy Week ALL DAYS located, two independent reconstructions found

Robinson's heavy/light two-week cycle is documented in unusual depth across multiple sources. Two different day-groupings exist across the secondary literature — both are given below since they don't fully agree on how many distinct sessions the "3-on/1-off" heavy week contains. All are Tier B/C (attributed quotes + secondary reconstruction of numbers); no single Tier A source gives the full numeric table.

### B3 (strongest, most primary-adjacent) — MuscleSportMag "Old School Workout No. 3"

https://www.musclesportmag.com/2017/06/30/old-school-workout-no-3-robby-robinsons-heavy-light/ — Tier B. Written by a lifter/coach who **trained alongside Robinson in person** ("I remember doing legs with him and we were on a light week and doing 10 plates a side on the leg press!!"), directly reproducing Robinson's heavy-week protocol as performed, not just reported secondhand. This is the closest thing to a primary account for Robinson found in this pass.

Light-week example given in the same source: Incline DB Press + Incline Cable Flyes supersetted for 3 sets (chest); Lunges, Sissy Squats, Leg Extensions, Leg Curls (legs) — deliberately unstructured, no fixed sets/reps by design.

### Reconciling B3/B1/B2 vs. B4

Both reconstructions agree on **exercise selection and the underlying pyramid philosophy** (light-to-heavy across 3 sets, or 4 straight sets in the higher-rep-range version) and are clearly built from the same underlying Robinson quotes — they disagree on **day grouping** (3 combined days vs. 6 separate days) and **set count** (3 vs. 4). Given B3's in-person account is the most primary-adjacent, the 3-day/3-set version (Chest+Back, Shoulders+Arms, Legs) should be treated as the more reliable day structure, with B4's separated-day version noted as an alternate secondary reconstruction, not a second attested routine.

### Updated verdict

Robby Robinson now has the most complete attested weekly training data of the five athletes researched in this pass — heavy week is fully covered across all major body parts (chest, back, shoulders, arms, legs, calves) with specific sets/reps, sourced from a person who trained alongside him plus multiple corroborating secondary write-ups containing direct quotes. Light week remains deliberately unstructured by design (confirmed, not a gap) — Robinson himself describes varying reps/sets/rest specifically to avoid adaptation.
