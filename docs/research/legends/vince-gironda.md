## Verified claims (Tier A/B only)

1. **Deliberate program variation ("Muscle Confusion"), in his own words (Iron Man, Nov 1983):** the body "does not respond to the same workout routine every workout session," so he intentionally rotated between named systems rather than running one fixed program тАФ listing 23 named variations including 3x8, 5x5, 6x6, 10x10, push/pull days, supersets, and body-part groupings.
2. **The 8x8 system is real and self-branded, from his own book** (*Unleashing the Wild Physique*, corroborated by Wikipedia/NSP Nutrition): 8 sets of 8 reps, called his "honest" program because lighter weight forces genuine muscle-focused work rather than ego lifting.
3. **Progressive beginner-to-8x8 ramp, in his own words (Wikipedia, quoting Gironda):** month 1 = 3x8, month 2 = 5x5, month 3 = 6x6 (retained at least 3 months), only then advance to 8x8.
4. **A real, named pupil's (Mohamed Makkawy) pre-contest 8x8 template:** 3 bodyparts/day, 3 exercises/bodypart, 8 sets/exercise = 24 sets/bodypart; 3 workouts of 2.5 hours each with 1 hour of rest between sessions on the same day; day 2 = arms & calves, day 3 = upper legs & abdominals (day 1's bodyparts not specified in the available source).
5. **Beginner full-body routine, documented in detail (Iron Man/Holman-Palmieri), with exact per-exercise rep counts:** 1) Decline pulley hugs (pectorals) 12 reps, 2) Short-pulley rows (upper lats/teres major) 12 reps, 3) Lateral raises (medial deltoid) 10 reps, 4) Triceps pushdowns 10 reps, 5) Barbell body-drag curls (biceps) 10 reps, 6) Seated wrist curls (forearms) 12 reps, 7) Leg extensions (thighs) 12 reps, 8) Leg curls (hamstrings) 12 reps, 9) Calf heel raises 20 reps, 10) Bent-knee leg raises (lower abs) 20+ reps, 11) Crunches (upper/middle abs) 10+ reps, 12) Double-ups (midsection) 10+ reps тАФ ramping from 1 to 3 sets/exercise over 4 weeks (week 4 also switches from daily to 3x/week), then held at 3 sets/exercise, 3x/week, for about 6 months before changing exercises. Exercises 10-12 are skipped for underweight trainees per Vince's own note.
6. **No barbell bench press or barbell back squat at Vince's Gym**, replaced by the neck/guillotine press (bar lowered to the throat, not the chest, on a Smith machine, done slowly) and wide dips for chest, and non-squat-rack leg work тАФ corroborated across the Iron Man beginner feature and Fitness Volt's tips round-up.
7. **No music in the gym, ever** тАФ his own stated reasoning (Iron Man feature, quoting him directly): sculpting the body requires "vivid imagination, enormous desire, total faith, visualization" that music disrupts.

**Summary:** anchor to *Unleashing the Wild Physique* (1984, his own book) for the 8x8 system's
existence and progression rule, and to the Iron Man Magazine beginner feature (drawing on Alan
Palmieri's documented compilation of his methods) for the fully-specified beginner routine тАФ since

**Principles (Tier A/B only):**
- Deliberately varied training systems over time ("muscle confusion") rather than running one fixed program тАФ rotated between 3x8, 5x5, 6x6, 10x10, and other named schemes.
- The 8x8 system (8 sets x 8 reps, light-enough weight, minimal rest) was his signature "honest" program, reached only after a beginner ramp: month 1 at 3x8, month 2 at 5x5, month 3 at 6x6 (held 3+ months) before attempting 8x8.
- Beginners train the same 12-exercise full-body routine daily (6 days/week) for the first month, ramping from 1 to 3 sets/exercise, then move to 3x/week and hold that routine roughly 6 months.
- Banned the plain barbell bench press and barbell back squat at his own gym; used the neck/guillotine press and wide dips for chest, and non-squat-rack alternatives for legs, prioritizing feeling the target muscle over moving maximum weight.
- No music in the gym тАФ training required total mental focus and visualization.

- `frequencyPerMuscle`: "Beginners: every training day for the first month, then every session on a 3x/week full-body plan. Advanced 8x8 (Makkawy example): each of 3 bodyparts trained once every 3-day cycle."

## Routine buildability

The beginner full-body routine maps onto `exercises.json` for 8 of its 12 exercises, at the
steady-state 3-sets/exercise phase (held ~6 months, per the article) and using the article's own
exact per-exercise rep counts: `seated-cable-row` 3x12 (short-pulley rows), `dumbbell-lateral-raise`
3x10 (lateral raises), `triceps-pushdown` 3x10, `barbell-curl` 3x10 (closest match to "barbell
body-drag curls" тАФ noted in the routine as an approximation since a true drag curl is a distinct
cue-set not yet in the library), `leg-extension` 3x12, `lying-leg-curl` 3x12 (leg curls),
`standing-calf-raise` 3x20 (calf heel raises), `decline-crunch` 3x"10+" (closest match to floor
crunches). "Decline pulley hugs" (decline cable flye), seated wrist curls, bent-knee leg raises,
and "double-ups" have no library match and are omitted (gap, not invented). 8 exercises at 3 sets
each, with sourced rep counts for every one, is enough for one legitimate legend routine. The
advanced 8x8/Makkawy template is not buildable into a routine without inventing which
exercises/bodyparts fill day 1 тАФ left as a documented principle only, not a routine.
