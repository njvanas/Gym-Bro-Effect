## Verified Claims (Tier B, unusually well cross-corroborated)

1. **A specific, directly-quoted, and independently-repeated (5x) volume/rep/rest protocol**: 30-50 sets per bodypart (16 for arms), twice weekly, 12-20 reps/set, 30 seconds rest between sets — explicitly stated by Nubret himself as the reason he "trained with only 70/100kg" despite a 225kg competition bench max, i.e., a deliberate choice to train light-for-him at high volume rather than a limitation (B1).
2. **A specific, quoted daily abdominal habit**: every morning, 7 days/week, working up to 2,000 continuous sit-ups — repeated consistently across every source reviewed, framed by Nubret as his substitute for traditional cardio, since his high-volume/low-rest weight training already provided the aerobic stimulus he needed (B1, corroborated across all secondary sources).
3. **A consistent, specific 6-day/week split hitting each muscle group twice** is reported identically across multiple independent sources (B2), consistent with B1's own "twice a week" quote.
4. **A specific, quoted diet detail**: up to 6 lbs of red meat/day during competition prep, alongside rice, beans, chicken, fish, vegetables (B1).
