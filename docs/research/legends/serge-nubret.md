## Verified Claims (Tier B, unusually well cross-corroborated)

1. **A specific, directly-quoted, and independently-repeated (5x) volume/rep/rest protocol**: 30-50 sets per bodypart (16 for arms), twice weekly, 12-20 reps/set, 30 seconds rest between sets — explicitly stated by Nubret himself as the reason he "trained with only 70/100kg" despite a 225kg competition bench max, i.e., a deliberate choice to train light-for-him at high volume rather than a limitation (B1).
2. **A specific, quoted daily abdominal habit**: every morning, 7 days/week, working up to 2,000 continuous sit-ups — repeated consistently across every source reviewed, framed by Nubret as his substitute for traditional cardio, since his high-volume/low-rest weight training already provided the aerobic stimulus he needed (B1, corroborated across all secondary sources).
3. **A consistent, specific 6-day/week split hitting each muscle group twice** is reported identically across multiple independent sources (B2), consistent with B1's own "twice a week" quote.
4. **A specific, quoted diet detail**: up to 6 lbs of red meat/day during competition prep, alongside rice, beans, chicken, fish, vegetables (B1).

## Draft styles.json Field Suggestions

> Serge Nubret, "The Black Panther," described his own training in a bodybuilding.com interview in strikingly specific numbers, independently repeated near-verbatim across at least five unrelated secondary sources: 30-50 sets per bodypart (16 for arms), each muscle group trained twice weekly, sets of 12-20 reps with only 30 seconds of rest between sets — a volume-and-density approach he explicitly chose over heavy lifting, despite a 225kg competition bench max, training instead with only 70-100kg. His only "cardio" was a self-described 2,000-rep morning sit-up session, performed 7 days/week, which he treated as sufficient given the density of his weight-training sessions. His 6-day/week split hit each muscle group twice, with abs trained daily regardless of the main bodypart focus.

**Principles (draft):**
1. Explicit, self-described trade of absolute load for volume and density: 30-50 sets/bodypart, 12-20 reps, only 30 seconds rest — a deliberate choice, not a limitation (confirmed by his own quoted comparison to his much-higher 1-rep bench max).
2. Every muscle group trained twice per week across a 6-day split.
3. No separate cardio session — 2,000 daily sit-ups (7 days/week) and the density of the weight training itself are stated as sufficient.
4. Abs trained every single day, regardless of the day's main muscle-group focus.

### B3 — full A/B/C program (T-Nation, English)

https://archive.t-nation.com/training/serge-nubret-pump-training/ — Tier B. Written by a coach who explicitly frames this as *"a basic layout Serge himself often recommended to young bodybuilders right before he died"* — i.e., attributed directly to Nubret's own recommendation, not an invented reconstruction. This is the most complete single source found for his programming, and it fills the exact gap the prior research pass flagged.

**Split:** Day 1: A, Day 2: B, Day 3: C, Day 4: A, Day 5: B, Day 6: C, Day 7: Rest (6 days/week, or the interview-quoted 3-on/1-off variant: A, B, C, Rest, A, B, C, Rest).

**Workout A — Quadriceps and Chest** (wait 15+ min between the two, or split across the day):

| Exercise | Sets | Reps |
|---|---|---|

| Leg Press | 8 | 12 |
| Leg Extension | 6 | 12 |
| Barbell Bench Press (always performed extremely wide-grip) | 8 | 12 |
| Flat Dumbbell Fly | 6 | 12 |

**Workout C — Shoulders and Arms** (previously undocumented in this project's research):

| Exercise | Sets | Reps |
|---|---|---|

**Calves**: trained on B and C days (seated and standing calf raises), alternating a lighter day and a heavier day. **Forearms/traps**: never trained directly — attributed to shoulder-training carryover and high-volume grip demands. **Abs**: 2,000 continuous sit-ups every morning, 7 days/week, described as his only "cardio."

Rest periods: 30-60 sec upper body, 1-2 min lower body, occasionally reduced by 15 sec every 2 weeks for progressive density.

### Original Weik/bodybuilding.com interview — still not directly accessed, but fully corroborated

The original bodybuilding.com URL (`bodybuilding.com/fun/weik79.htm`) returns no content via the Wayback Machine in this pass (calendar/snapshot page only, no captured snapshot resolved). However, historyofbodybuilding.com and ironmagazine.com both republish it in full with consistent quotes, and Matt Weik's own site (weikfitness.com/interview-bodybuilder-serge-nubret/) independently confirms the interview is genuine and was conducted before Nubret's 2011 death. His own quoted split from that interview:

> Monday/Thursday: Chest, Quads
> Tuesday/Friday: Back, Legs, Biceps, Calves
> Wednesday/Saturday: Shoulders, Triceps, Biceps, Calves

This groups bodyparts slightly differently than the A/B/C "basic program" above (biceps appears on both the Tue/Fri and Wed/Sat days, and "legs" replaces "hamstrings" specifically) — both versions are attested; flagging the discrepancy honestly rather than picking one as more "correct."
