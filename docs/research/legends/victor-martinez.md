## Draft styles.json Field Suggestions

> Victor Martinez, "The Dominican Dominator," is one of the best-quoted names in this research batch, with consistent, cross-corroborated principles across at least four separate interviews spanning 2007-2020s. His defining, most-repeated principle is prioritization: whatever bodypart is the day's main focus is trained first, while freshest (hamstrings before quads, incline before flat bench, side laterals before other shoulder work) — explicitly for pre-exhaustion and targeted development. He learned proper full-range-of-motion form by directly studying Arnold Schwarzenegger's technique in *Pumping Iron*. His training frequency is a stated, flexible 5 days/week (3-on/2-off, 4-on/1-off, or 5 straight, chosen by how he feels that week), with calves every 2 days and abs explicitly capped at 2x/week because more makes them grow larger than he wants aesthetically. A detailed, dated current routine (Muscle Insider feature) documents specific weights/sets/reps across a 4-day rotating split, and confirms he trains partly at Bev Francis's Powerhouse Gym in Syosset, NY.

**Principles (draft):**
1. Explicit "prioritize" rule: the day's target bodypart is always trained first, while freshest, for maximum pre-exhaustion/focus.
2. Full range of motion, learned directly by studying Arnold's form in *Pumping Iron* — never uses a weight he can't move through the complete range.
3. Flexible 5-day/week frequency, with the exact on/off pattern chosen weekly based on how he feels, not a fixed calendar.
4. Calves trained every 2 days; abs capped at 2x/week specifically to avoid over-developing them.
5. Does all the basic compound lifts (bench, squat, deadlift) but sequences them deliberately within a session rather than always leading with them (e.g., squats last on leg day).
