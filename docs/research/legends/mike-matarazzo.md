## Draft styles.json Field Suggestions

> Mike Matarazzo, "The Boston Mass," is best documented for two specific, named body parts: calves and arms. His calf training combined heavy loading (failing well under 10 reps), a self-described "controlled cheating" technique to overcome the calf's leverage disadvantage at full stretch, and deliberate sequencing directly after hamstrings to use existing blood flow — plus a personal habit, carried over from boxing, of staying on his toes throughout the day. For arms, he directly credits Larry Scott with personally teaching him the sideways one-arm dumbbell preacher curl early in his pro career, a technique he used for the rest of his career.

**Principles (draft):**
1. "Controlled cheating" on calves: strict form at the start of a set, momentum used only to clear the sticking point at full stretch, never at the end.
2. Calves trained immediately after hamstrings to exploit existing blood flow.
3. A boxing-derived, all-day habit (staying on the toes) is credited as contributing to calf development, not just gym work.
4. Sideways one-arm dumbbell preacher curls, learned directly from Larry Scott early in his pro career, became a permanent fixture of his arm training.
