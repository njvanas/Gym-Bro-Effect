## Verified Claims (Tier B, cross-corroborated across 4 independent, years-apart interviews)

1. **A specific, named, and consistently self-described training system ("push-pull," in use "since 1984") is directly quoted across at least 3 separate interviews spanning 2002-2014** (B1, B2, B3) — an unusually strong level of cross-corroboration for a named personal system in this batch.
2. **A specific, quoted weekly structure**: Push (chest/shoulders/triceps) Mon/Thu, Pull (back/biceps/traps) Tue/Fri, a second Push block (legs/abs) Wed/Sat, often training all 7 days (B2).
3. **A specific, quoted 2002 comeback protocol**: 6 days/week, 2 sessions/day (~1.5 hrs each), 3 exercises/bodypart, 5 sets/exercise, 10-12 reps (good day) to 6-8 (bad day), with an explicit "don't chase your old size" philosophy (B1).
4. **A specific, quoted biceps-training cap**: moderate-to-medium-heavy weight only, "anything above that would be overtraining," max 5×10-12 (B3).
5. **A dated, specific, and verifiable injury-and-invention origin story** for his VT/Precision Grips product, directly in his own words, including the exact mechanism of discovery (B4).
6. **A second, later-career named system ("J.R.P. & GRIPS") is directly quoted as being actively developed** as of 2014 (B2) — worth flagging as a distinct, later addition to his push-pull framework, not a replacement for it.
