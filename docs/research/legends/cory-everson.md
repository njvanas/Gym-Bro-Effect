## 5. Draft summary + principles + guidelines (grounded only in Tier B direct quotes)

### Summary (draft)
Six-time, undefeated Ms. Olympia (1984-89). Her training approach — as she has described it directly in first-person interviews — followed a rolling push/legs/pull/rest 4-day cycle (chest-shoulders-triceps, then legs, then back-biceps, then a day off) borrowed from a popular 1980s template, with abs trained separately 3-4 times a week regardless of that cycle. She and then-husband/trainer Jeff Everson tracked "guide lifts" (the heaviest exercise in each session — squats, bench press, barbell rows) as her core progress benchmark. No transcribed numeric log from her own 1991 book or self-produced training video was located this pass; every set/rep number here comes from her own later, directly-quoted retrospective interviews, not the book/video text itself.

### Principles (draft)
- Rolling push (chest/shoulders/triceps) → legs → pull (back/biceps) → rest, 4-day cycle, repeated continuously.
- Abs trained 3-4x/week as their own category, independent of the push/legs/pull cycle.
- "Guide lifts" (squats, bench press, barbell rows) tracked explicitly for weight/rep progress as the benchmark for whether the whole program is working.
- Free-weight-first: deliberately avoided over-reliance on machines, contrary to the era's stereotype about women bodybuilders.
- Deliberate middle-delt emphasis for shoulder width, achieved through exercise selection (side laterals, upright rows, behind-the-neck press) rather than just adding volume.
- Explicit, named overtraining vigilance — she describes concrete signs and a fixed response (time off), not just "listen to your body."

### Guidelines (draft)
- **Training cycle:** Push/Legs/Pull/Rest, repeating every 4 days (not a fixed 7-day week).
- **Rep range (most bodyparts):** 15-to-8 descending pyramid across working sets, aiming for at least 8 reps on the heaviest set.
- **Squats/guide lifts:** 8 total sets per leg session (3 warm-up + 5 working), pyramided 15→8 reps.
- **Calves:** 3 exercises, 3 sets each, 15-25 reps.
- **Abs:** giant sets of 3 exercises (upper/lower/full-ab), 20-30 reps each, 3-4 giant sets, 3-4x/week.
- **Shoulders:** 3 sets of 15-8 reps for laterals, upright rows, and behind-neck presses, with deliberate middle-delt bias.
