## Verified Claims (Tier B, cross-corroborated across at least 2 independent interviews)

1. **An explicit, quoted philosophy of deliberately varying his routine** rather than following a fixed program — exercise order, set count, and rest periods all change between sessions, by his own description (B1).
2. **A specific, quoted frequency pattern**: quads once/week, hamstrings twice/week, with priority-based AM/PM ordering when both are trained same day (B1).
3. **An explicit, quoted lagging-bodypart protocol with two named options**: (a) increase frequency to 2-3x/week while reducing sets/exercise but raising intensity, or (b) split total weekly volume evenly across 3 sessions — plus a self-described overtraining warning sign (chronic fatigue) and remedy (drop back to 2x/week) (B1).
4. **An explicit, repeated, and consistently-quoted philosophy across two separate interviews (B1, B2) of deliberately choosing free weights over machines** specifically because they are harder/more painful, not because they're objectively superior for everyone — with the same underlying "train to failure to force growth" logic repeated in both (B1, B2, corroborated by B3).
5. **5-6 days/week training frequency (once/day) in the off-season**, with twice-daily cardio added during contest prep, but weight training frequency otherwise unchanged pre-contest (B2).

## Gaps

- No specific sets/reps table for a full workout (beyond the general "3-4 exercises/bodypart" framing in one lower-confidence secondary source) was found directly quoted — his own words consistently emphasize variability over fixed numbers, so a fixed sets/reps table would arguably misrepresent his stated philosophy.
- No warm-up protocol was found in his own words.
